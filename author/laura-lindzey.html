Working at Progress Airfield

January 10, 2016

I spent this field season as a member of CHINARE 32, supporting the operation of their brand-new airplane that is being used to collect geophysical data about Antarctica. This airplane operated out of the Progress runway, which is a rugged 30-60 minute drive from the main station. In order to make the logistics simpler, Chinare set up a camp for the fixed-wing team right next to the runway. This was fantastically convenient - whenever the airplane was flying, the whole science team was able to live up there. Read on →
